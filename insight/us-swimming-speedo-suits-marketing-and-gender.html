In this article ❧ speedo's strategy is safe but it is lagging behind market trends. We analyze the gender and design strategy in products Speedo for boys and girls.

The design of this report did not start in the usual way. Usually, the Hexis team meets to update our publishing strategy. However, the idea of writing about Speedo came up in one of the brand's stores . We were not in a business visit, or researching (we would like that): we were buying swimming accessories for one of us: of us needed to buy something for his daughter. And it was in that context, examining tags prices, fins, sticky shoes, shark caps, magnifying glasses (yes,there are magnifying glasses for swimming for people with myopia). Seeing all those things, the idea came up, the idea of doing a little study on Speedo Product Design Strategy , and more specifically about possible feelings that its gender strategy could cause in parents.

Ethnography: the busiest pool in Bogotá

How to get two mothers of middle or upper middle class, who have sons or daughters small, who practice swimming, and were they going to collaborate in our research? The question had a concrete answer: Compensar, Olimpic swimming pools of the CUR , in Bogotá. This place is probably the most popular in Bogotá at the time to swim. Every day dozens - maybe hundreds - of children take classes, so is was going to not be difficult to know persons like the ones we wanted to interview.

Stage: What are the swimsuit for?

The aquatic complex has two large areas. We chose the "Olympic 1" area, as it is the most complex area (it houses three swimming pools).

Each of the three pools mentioned has a specialty: older adults, classes of swimming and playing.

We discard the first one because only incidentally there are children in it. In the second (a swimming pool divided laterally into lanes of 25 meters) there is a lot of children's activity. Groups between 5 to 20 children receive classes led by uniformed instructors.

Insight

Speedo : We have seen that there are two audiences and that each one can be served with a relative different idea. On one hand, we have buyers who are not very sensitive to gender ideas; they could be parents of children; on the other side, we have fathers and mothers (especially mothers with a certain educational profile) interested in gender equity.

Swimming suits in economically differentiated segments

The ages of the students range from 4 to 15 years. Their swimming suits follow a clear logic: the students who come from public schools wear generic suits, no particular brand, and, in somecases, garments that are not even for swimming. In contrast, students who pay for private classes use brand costumes; the special design does not seem to have any impact on sports practice (moreover: the attention that swimming demands it is such that only a trained eye could recognize such distinctions)

    Profile for the focus group

  • Age 25 to 45 years.
  • Parents of children who go to the pool at least twice a month.
  • With university studies.
  • With an observable, middle-class lifestyle.
  • Who knew brands like Speed or Arena.

Results of a focus group

Josefina and Erica - our two interviewees - are mothers of a 6-year-old boy (swimmis five times a week and takes part of the Compensar team) and a 9-year-old girl (also a member of the swimming teams). The first adore Speedo's designs, the second stopped buying the brand for not matching their ideas about equity of gender

Business opportunities in the children and sports segment

What is the value proposition in Speedo's design? Simply, it's about delivering quality in materials, comfort, colors, textures, safety, and even fun (if we consider, for example the creation of a visual identity based on a proposal of cartooning for packaging.That is what is visible.But in Speedo's value proposition there is also a meta-discourse, a message whose meaning makes sense when the consumer connects it with more or less generalized social models of thinking. Speedo raises a clear message: boys are tough and girls are tender . And there is a problem. Well the mindset of the Middle-class consumers - increasingly educated and more cosmopolitan- no longer associate kids with something active and girls with something passive.
