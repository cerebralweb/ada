We Interviewed the Former Carrefour marketing Director

Paulo César León Palacios
Listen

In this article ❧ THe Episode 4 of our podcast is dedicated to chatting with Jeisson Hurtado, Marketing Director for Mario Hernández, the number one Colombian brand of leather products.

Jeisson studied marketing and advertising at Politecnico Gran Colombiano and before working at Mario Hernández, he led marketing processes at Carrefour and Cencosud (in the latter he spent 6 years and led the development of digital marketing).

At Carrefour

When talking about his career, Jeisson begins with a brand that I remember well. Buying food in Carrefour was nice: the facilities were the largest available in supermarkets at the time (about 15 years ago), its colors (the same as the French flag) projected confidence and the food was not bad for a supermarket in Bogota. Perhaps the latter was the most notable thing about this brand, as Carrefour managed to sell the idea that good quality food was available to a wider audience (thus, it differed from the old Carulla, supermarket brand focused on kind "gourmet" food market). I am not surprised that when reviewing the recent actions of this brand I find, for example, that Carrefour has led campaigns to improve the quality of food in France and the European Union.

For 3 years, says Jeisson, Carrefour worked on the development of a digital sales platform, something not seen in Colombia at the time. Then its supermarkets in Colombia were sold to Cencosud. Jeisson then became involved in the management of Jumbo and Metro, 2 brands of the new owner.

What concept of digital marketing did they have at Carrefour

It was a very basic concept. It all came down to installing an e-commerce platform and the idea of ​​communicating with the customer did not yet exist.

There was no contact with the end customer from digital channels. There was an institutional page (...) social networks were not there yet. From the marketing area, communication channels with clients were not managed either.

The impact of the Internet on Colombian marketing

I asked Jeisson if the Internet has had a revolutionary impact in a market like Colombia:

The Internet has become an infinite source of knowledge (...) at the business level, now brands have to think about how to approach customers where they are. (...) (Additionally) In Colombia the screen time on smartphone is 3.5 hours.

Do you have experience with online communities?

For the past few months, Jeisson has been working in an online Mario Hernández community based on blogging, email marketing, and leather related activities.

Developing a community around fashion is very interesting. We are currently working in a VIP customer club where a community of people who love leather is grouped, how we are teaching them how to combine colors, what materials to use. We have seen how people connect, we have a fashion editor. In the first month of launch alone, we had more than 15,000 contacts and registered users (...) invited them to talks, breakfasts, tours of the factory.

How have you connected this to market research?

The brand is currently working on a qualitative study with Nielsen, focused on understanding the purchasing drivers of the segments interested in brands such as Mario Hernández.

When I asked Jeisson what these companies are looking for in market research, a simple but powerful answer came up. The most important thing is that the proposed methodologies and solutions coincide with the business objectives that motivate companies to carry out market studies.

I was surprised that the processes to evaluate market research proposals do not weigh upon personal relationships but the results of tenders with suppliers.

The agency must interpret the business objectives well and meet the evaluation criteria from the purchasing area.
