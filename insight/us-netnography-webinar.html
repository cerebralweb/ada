Netnography can tackle consumer reluctance to participate in marketing research

Paulo César León Palacios

Consumers are less and less interested in participating in market research. Netnography can help In the United States, for example, the Telephone interview response rate fell from 36% in 1997 to 9% in 2012. Survey response rates They have also declined in both the United States and Europe. Probably one of the causes is that Digital is increasingly important in consumers' lifestyles, and therefore the expectation to participate in traditional research activities may be lower: traditional research must be adjusted to this, and netnography is an option.

What is netnography?

Netnography was introduced in the late 1990s by Robert Kozinets. The word Netnography is a neologism built from the InterNET words and ethNOGRAPHY. . Researchers dive deep into attitudes, behaviors, preferences, needs and ideas of consumers. Netnography is currently used in all industries and for a variety of questions from investigation.

A case of the successful use of netnography in the cosmetics industry here .

In the last 15 years, the development of netnography has gained momentum in both the academic and business worlds. Companies like BMW, Gore or Beiersdorf carried out the first projects applied to marketing and retail.
