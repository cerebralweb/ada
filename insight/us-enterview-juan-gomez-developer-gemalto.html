"The only companies that are generating good jobs are software companies"

Paulo César León Palacios
Escuchar

In this article ❧ The Episode 1 of SmallData -the Hexis podcast- is dedicated to talking with Juan Sebastián Gómez, a Colombian developer specialized in mobile applications and computer security.

Sebastián studied systems engineering at the Colombian School of Engineering and has a 10-year career as developer. Currently, he works for Gemalto --Thales consortium-- among other companies.

In the interview, Sebastián tells his beginnings as a videogame developer (as part of his undergraduate thesis), and how that led him to work with computer security companies.

Then, he got involved in Preo, a New York startup that developed an app to buy beers in bars. The business model was based on the app being able to scan receipts paid by consumers and redeem them for discounts.

Preo failed:

If there is no good business model, in the end it will not work no matter how hard you try the technical part

During his work at Preo (his position was equivalent to a Technical Manager), Sebastián learned that the way of development of the North Americans (the development, originally, came from New York) was very different from that of the Colombian developers:

American developers developed very slowly (...) there they are more methodical. They do not realize that it is a startup and that there are some steps to skip

But, at the same time, Sebastián explains that the business (which consisted of promoting the consumption of more beer using the app and scanning discount coupons) was also not understood by the Colombian counterpart. In his view, local entrepreneurs wanted immediate profits, even if that went against the proper development of the business model.

In the conversation on the subject, Rappi, is mentioned, as the company where the Product Mananger in Bogotá came from. The Product Manager's strategies did not work, and eventually the startup ran out of investors.

I knew that if they let us put developers here in Colombia, we could do the development 3 times faster than them (...) I managed to convince them, I managed to get a friend with whom I have always worked (...) The new product manager who came from Rappi also agreed with that (...) the server was working on the side of the people of the United States at a slow speed. On our side we were not worried, we knew that we could solve many things using cloud tools (...) the North Americans began to do code reviews and put problems, but many of the code problems were their own problems.

American developers are still good at building big things (...) for fast things they're not, less when we talking about Latin American businesses. For example, Latin American banks move very differently from American banks, they want to see everything quickly; here from one moment to the next they are putting biometrics, etc ... they are always trying to put the last, the last; things get unpredictable, and in the end there was never a growth curve for people to learn.

The interview also talks about BBC, a Colombian beer brand owned by the Belgian multinational AB InBev. Initially, Preo was offered to the consortium. Essentially, Preo allowed bar patrons to purchase beer from the app; the promise was that consumption would increase. AB InBev decided to experiment with Preo with the Colombian BBC; with the results that Juan has told in the interview.

Later in the podcast we talked about the ways to categorize developers, how is it like to manage technical tests to enter into a technology company and what difficulties run into those who follow this path

Finally, we talk about how to make music apps and how the app market works.

We have discovered that the theme of music, of music apps, is not a massive theme. While in the United States we are talking about 30,000 downloads, here in Colombia we are talking about 100 downloads . There are very few people who can live well from this in Colombia. That does not allow people to have the leisure to make music.

Another conclusion of Juan's ventures is that it is extremely difficult for other developers to commit themselves if there are no immediate rewards. Hence, John preferred to associate only with his brother.

On a technical level, we are talking about React Native . Juan's opinion is that this technology is not suitable for high-performance apps: in the end the developer will have to learn the native technologies. In Juan's case, for example, he had to use Swift's native music engine.

What do you think about the relationship between mathematics and development?

Necessary. Poorly taught, but necessary (...) I only learned to pass the subject (...) It would be more prudent to put people to build things and there you go looking for what you need (...) the universities in the subject are not very good. They put you to work on the easy problems and leave the difficult most for the final exams
