Airbnb Insights

Paulo César León Palacios

In this article ❧ Airbnb has a fortune in data. They come from the 200 million people who have booked trips on their platform and the 3 million users whose properties offer on the Internet. Despite this, the brand firmly believes that qualitative research is essential for the development of its products.

The Airbnb Insights Machine

Judd Antin - director of research for the brand - is in charge of the interesting task of converting shopping experiences into actionable insights for Airbab. Antin works with 57 experts who combine large sets of quantitative information with qualitative information. The strategic goal of insights is to "deeply understand our global guests and hosts well enough to make decisions", Antin said at the 2017 Strategy Festival of the American Association of Advertising Agencies. In his presentation, Antin talked about two projects that demonstrate how his Insights Department helps the company.

Judd Antin

Judd Antin is itself an interesting topic. On reading his linkedin, his scientific training is obvious: he completed a doctorate in information systems and studied psychology and anthropology; The latter is his basic training. The Airbab Research Department is divided into three wings. Survey Science , obviously related to insight extraction from large data sets; User Experience (UX), which investigates the human computer relationship with digital ethnography techniques and Product Specialists who are dedicated to translating insights into specific strategies and solutions.

Guest hosting

In one of the studies Antin and his team analyzed the company's host tools, which include: 1) answers to frequently asked questions, 2) calendars to organize reservation dates, 3) a control panel that reports host data (earnings, views and ratings, among others) and 4) price advice.

The problem is that many of these tools are difficult to understand and use. The task was, then, to analyze user behavior data to decipher what and how to improve.

The research was not limited to large quantitative data. A team traveled to strategic places, such as the United States, to conduct ethnographic field work. Thus, they learned about the feelings of those who offer their homes for Airbnb service.

The guests

A "check-in-along" study in New York, Bangkok and Beijing allowed the brand to know what happens when guests first arrive at their chosen accommodation:

We wanted to understand - Antin explained - how was being a guest like, when you show up on an Airbnb: we join the guests in those countries to see what happens.

The deep knowledge extracted from a small sample of users proved to be essential to complement the available user statistical data.

"We are disorderly animals" ... and that's why we need ethnography

Antin's theory went further. In his perspective it is pertinent to integrate Big Data with qualitative research :

We want to get great answers and we certainly fight to build a great strategy. And we know that, to do that, a truly holistic perspective is required (...) Traveling is cross-cultural. In a large proportion of stays, the hosts and guests on Airbnb speak a different first language. Just think on that alone as a necessity to have a global perspective of humans. It is not only what clicks can tell us, but humans what can they tell us (...) Data is powerful, but it has no soul. It is necessary, but insufficient, for a good strategy and good products.

And continued:

Data and data science have a lot of information (...) They really have a rich and detailed picture of what people are doing. How? and because? It is another matter. In fact, I think the data (logging data, behavior data) by themselves does not tell us much about how things happen, why they happen. If you want to know how? and Why? You need more than data (...) We are in a design-driven organization in which research is a leading voice, and our mission, which is about belonging and trust, genuinely guides us. Those are human things. We are messy animals, complex and volatile; Airbnb, therefore, needs a full range of methodology to understand these nuances.

Findings

As always, some crucial observations arise here.

  1. Judd Antin's training is a mixture of data science, anthropology and psychology.
  2. For companies seeking actionable insights it is crucial to decipher how to combine Big Data with qualitative data
  3. In other words: the future is to combine data science with anthropology, sociology, history and psychology.
  4. ... and the future is also, of course, knowing how to combine all that into plans orchestrated by creative consultants.
